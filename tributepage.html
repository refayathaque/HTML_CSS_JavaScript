Timeline of Jamie Dimon's life :

  • 1956: Jamie Dimon is born to a Greek family in New York City, and eventually becomes a third-generation stockbroker. Finance was the family business, both Jamie's father and grandfather were stockbrokers. His formative years were spent between Park Avenue and Westchester, NY.
  • 1978: Graduates from Tufts University.
  • 1982: Graduates from Harvard Business School with an MBA.
  • 1982: After graduation, starts working for Sandy Weill, a family friend and Dimon's mentor. He works as Sandy's assistant at American Express.
  • 1986: He follows Sandy out of American Express, and into the consumer lending company Commercial Credit. He was Sandy's right-hand man at Commercial Credit, and they achieved several M&As. Soon after, the duo integrated Primerica, Smith Barney and Travelers Group.
  • 1996: He is named Chairman and CEO of Smith Barney, and undertakes many more acquisitions.
  • 1997: Salomon Brothers joins the group, and by the end of the decade Jamie becomes Chairman and co-CEO of Salomon Smith Barney Holdings.
  • 1998: Spring sees the merger of Travelers Group and Citicorp, which gives birth to Citigroup. Jamie becomes the President of Citigroup.
  • 1998: Fall sees the resignation of newly appointed Jamie from the President's post at Citigroup. A personal conflict with Sandy, due to his growing displeasure over Jamie's rise in authority and stature, leads to Jamie's resignation.
  • 2000: Jamie joins Bank One and becomes its Chairman and CEO. Bank One, a Chicago based firm, was undergoing severe financial difficulties at the time. In the subsequent three years Jamie turned things around, and repaired the firm's balance sheets.
  • 2004: He leads Bank One's merger with JPMorgan Chase, and becomes President and COO of JPMorgan Chase.
  • 2006: He assumes the role of CEO at JPMorgan Chase.
  • 2008: The financial crisis hits, and Jamie steers JPMorgan Chase clear of most of the danger. During this tumultous period he manages to keep the firm profitable. And in the process comes out acquiring the failing investment bank Bear Stearns at $2 a share, an eye-watering 7% of what the company was valued at just days before.
  • 2012: "London Whale": JPMorgan Chase's London trading arm suffers $6b in losses. The firm capitulates to $1b in regulatory fines after this debacle. Jamie himself gets a pay cut as a result of this incident.
  • 2013: JPMorgan Chase is accused of misrepresenting mortgage securities pre 2008 financial crisis. Reconciliation efforts result in a $13b settlement with the US government.
  • 2014: Despite the $13b settlement from the year prior, Jamie still secures a 74% raise in pay year-on-year. News about his throat cancer emanates, but he recovers and is back at the helm of the investment bank the following year.

Information above retrieved from Biography
