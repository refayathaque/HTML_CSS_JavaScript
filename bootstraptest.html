Timeline of Jamie Dimon's life :

  • 1956: Jamie Dimon is born in New York City, and eventually becomes a third-generation stockbroker. His formative years were spent between Park Avenue and Larchmont in Westchester County, NY. Finance was family business, both Jamie's father and grandfather were stockbrokers.
  • 1978: Jamie graduates from Tufts University.
  • 1982: Jamie graduates from Harvard Business School with an MBA.
  • 1982: Jamie starts working for Sandy Weill, a friend of the company, and Jamie's mentor. He works as Sandy's assistant at American Express.
  • 1986: Jamie follows Sandy out of American Express to the consumer lending company Commercial Credit Company. Jamie was Sandy's right-hand man at Commercial Credit,and they achieved several M&As. Soon the duo integrated Primerica, Smith Barney and Travelers Group.
  • 1996: Jamie is named Chairman and CEO of Smith Barney, and undertakes many more acquisitions.
  • 1997: Salomon Brothers joins the Jamie's group, and the end of the decade he becomes Chairman and co-CEO of Salomon Smith Barney Holdings.
  • 1998: Spring sees the merger of Travelers Group and Citicorp gives birth to Citigroup, and Jamie becomes the bank's President.
  • 1998: Fall sees the resignation of newly appointed Jamie from the President's post. Personal conflict with Sandy, due to his growing displeasure over Jamie's rise in authority and stature feeds the animosity between the two, and eventually to Jamie's resignation.
  • 2000: Jamie joins Bank One and becomes their Chairman and CEO. Bank One, a Chicago based firm, was undergoing severe financial difficulties. For the next three years Jamie repaired Bank One's balance sheets.
  • 2004: Jamie organizes Bank One's merger with JPMorgan Chase, and becomes the President and COO of JPMorgan Chase.
  • 2006: Jamie assumes the role of CEO at JPMorgan Chase.
  • 2008: The financial crisis hits, and Jamie steers JPMorgan Chase clear of most of the danger, and remarkably keeps the firm profitable. And in the process he comes out acquiring the failing Bear Stearns at $2 a share, 7 percent of what the company was valued just days before.
  • 2012: "London Whale", JPMorgan Chase's London trading arm suffers $6b in losses. The firm is forced to conceded $1b in regulatory fines after this debacle. Jamie himself gets a pay cut as a result of this incident.
  • 2013: JPMorgan Chase accused of misrepresenting mortgage securities pre 2008 financial crisis. Reconciliation efforts sees a $13b settlement with the US government.
  • 2014: Despite the settlement payout from the year prior, Jamie still secures a 74 percent rise in pay over what he made in 2013. During this year news emanates of his throat cancer, he recovers and is back at the helm of the investment bank the following year.
